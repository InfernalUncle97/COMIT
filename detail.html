Project Context

Saibai Island is a small island located in the Torres Strait, around 5 km from Papua New Guinea and over 140 km from the Australian mainland. The island faces a lot of issues due to its geographical position, vulnerability to coastal flooding and a reliance on imported diesel fuel for electricity generation (Engineers Without Borders Australia [EWB], 2024). This dependency not only makes the island's energy supply costly and unreliable, but also contributes to global warming and hampers Saibai's energy autonomy.

With the increasing frequency of flooding, and the island‚Äôs limited land availability, there is a serious need to explore alternative energy options that are sustainable, reliable, and climate-resilient. Access to a dependable renewable power supports and maintains the overall quality of life for the Saibai community.

Design Area and Project Opportunity

Our group has selected Design Area 4.1 ‚Äì Energy from the EWB Challenge Design Brief.

Within this area, our chosen Project Opportunity focuses on developing an innovative renewable energy generation and storage system tailored to Saibai Island‚Äôs environmental conditions. The proposed solution aims to integrate clean energy production with flood resilience and minimal land disturbance (EWB, 2024).

Project Overview

Electricity generated from the PV modules on the floating platforms, is transmitted through underwater cables to an onshore dual battery and power converter unit. It is mounted on stilts to protect it from tidal surges and flooding, with the configuration allowing for energy to be both stored and converted efficiently for community use, ensuring reliable access even during adverse weather or nighttime hours (Go Be Solar, n.d.; RWE Research & Development, n.d.).

Needs Statement

Saibai Island requires a sustainable and resilient energy system that:

  • Reduces dependence on imported diesel fuel.
  • Provides continuous and reliable power to meet community needs.
  • Withstands flooding, tidal fluctuations, and climate-related challenges.
  • Utilizes available land space effectively, preserving valuable land for other purposes.
  • Supports the island‚Äôs long-term environmental and economic sustainability. (EWB, 2024).

Our project addresses these needs by proposing a floating solar-based renewable energy solution that aligns with local environmental conditions, community priorities, and EWB‚Äôs goals for sustainable development in the Torres Strait.

Project Significance

This initiative is important, as it directly contributes to Saibai Island‚Äôs energy autonomy. By putting together solar generation with floating infrastructure, the solution minimises land use, mitigates flood risk and offers a scalable approach to renewable power generation (Hicks et al., 2022).

In doing so, it supports the EWB Challenge‚Äôs broader objectives. These involve empowering remote communities through carefully designed engineering solutions that integrate with the local community and have the capacity to cater to their needs, while also promoting environmental stewardship and long-term self-sufficiency (EWB, 2024).
