First Prototype - Lego Model

The first model our group created was a simple lego prototype to demonstrate the general appearance of the finished floating solar array. It is constructed out of lego blocks, making scale hard to determine, however it is useful as a visual aid to demonstrate the modularity of the design and the way which it connects to the electrical grid on Saibai Island. Testing of the prototype as a visual aid took place in person showing the design to other challenge participants. The model failed to effectively communicate the scale of the project which was a main point of feedback received.

Second Prototype - 3D Model

The prototype to the right, a 3D model, improves upon the previous visual aid constructed out of lego in order to showcase how the final floating solar array might look on the coast of Saibai Island. Compared to the lego model, the 3D Model gives a better sense of scale with an increase in the number of pontoon modules, although this is still a small section of what the full system would take up. The prototype is unsuitable for practical design testing as it serves purely as a visualisation of the floating solar project.

Design Drawings

The design drawings above showcase our considerations of the functional elements of the floating solar panel project. The left image highlights the connections between solar pontoon modules and nearby buoys to moor the pontoon array close to the shore of Saibai Island. As can be seen in the bottom left figure, each of the pontoon platforms will have multiple connection points to allow for the linking of the platforms and the mooring system. The right image models the details of the mooring system and a more in-depth look at the construction of the floating platforms. As in the image power from the floating solar array will be transferred onto the island by using an underwater water-proof power cable which aggregates the energy generated by the solar platform array. Each of the platforms will be mounted either to multiple other platforms, or to platforms and a buoy to ensure that the system does not drift away from its designated position.
