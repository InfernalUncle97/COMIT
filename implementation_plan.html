Implementation Plan

The floating solar project will be built step by step with help from the Saibai Island community. The plan focuses on teaching local people how to build, maintain, and manage the system over time, creating jobs and skills in the community.

Construction & Community Training

The system will use 10×10 m floating platforms made from HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) with solar panels mounted on top. Construction will happen close to the shore, where the platforms can be assembled and pushed into the water.

Local workers can help assemble and anchor the platforms on the water. They will receive basic training on how to:

  • Assemble and connect the floats, frames, and panels
  • Install waterproof cables and mooring ropes
  • Monitor energy data and maintain the system

Training will be done through workshops with step-by-step demonstrations. This helps create local skills for long-term maintenance.

Installation Stages

The system will be built and expanded over several years.

  • First Year: 20 floating platforms will be installed and main infrastructure (battery, Underwater Cable, Anchoring points and etc) will be installed
  • Second Year: Another 20 Platforms will be added
  • Third Year: Another 20 platforms will be added (the number may vary depending on demand)
  • Future years: Additional floating platforms will be added based on demand.

Over time, the total number of platforms may be reduced. As efficiency of solar panels improves and battery system expected to be cheaper, the same amount of energy could be produced with fewer platforms.

This flexible plan allows the system to grow or adjust based on future technology and community needs.

Operation and Maintenance

After installation, the system will be managed by trained local operators.

  • Regularly cleaning the solar panels
  • Checking anchors and cables
  • Using weather sensors and a 4G data logger to monitor performance
  • Detaching and reattaching platforms when needed
  • Relocates platforms

If a platform or cable is damaged, it can be easily repaired or replaced without affecting the entire system.

This project will also create new job opportunities on the island.

Evaluation

The project’s success will be measured through several key indicators:

  • The amount of energy generated each month
  • The number of diesel generators replaced
  • The reduction in fuel use and carbon emission
  • Community feedback and satisfaction levels
  • The number of local jobs created during and after construction

Reports will be shared with local authorities and the Torres Strait Island Regional Council (TSIRC) every six months.

Future & Ownership

Once complete, the project will supply most of Saibai Island’s electricity needs.

The community will own and manage the solar system with support from government agencies.

Because the design is modular, new platforms can be added easily in the future.

If any part breaks, replacement floats or cables can be installed quickly without removing the whole system.

Long-Term Impact

  • Clean, renewable power and less pollution
  • New local jobs and training opportunities
  • Lower energy costs over time
  • Better energy security during storms and power outages
  • Model project for other Torres Strait and remote island communities
  • More reliable electricity supply
